# Instagram Growth Strategy for accepted.fyi

## Instagram vs TikTok Comparison

| Factor | TikTok | Instagram Reels |
|--------|--------|----------------|
| **Organic reach for new accounts** | Higher — algorithm pushes content regardless of followers | Lower — favors accounts with existing followers |
| **Clickable link in bio** | Need 1K followers | Available immediately |
| **Links in DMs** | Restricted | Work perfectly |
| **Links in comments** | Not clickable | Not clickable |
| **Audience for admissions content** | Huge — students live here | Smaller but parents are more active here |
| **Virality potential** | Higher — one video can blow up with 0 followers | Harder to go viral from zero |

### Summary

- **TikTok** is better for **views and reach**
- **Instagram** is better for **driving traffic to your site** (clickable bio link from day one)

## Strategy: Post on Both Platforms

Post the same exact video on both platforms. Export from Canva/CapCut, upload to TikTok, then upload the same video to Instagram Reels.

### Where to Focus Energy

- **TikTok** for discovery — getting in front of new people
- **Instagram** for conversion — actually getting people to click through to your site
- In TikTok videos, say "find us on Instagram" as a workaround until 1K followers on TikTok

## Instagram Bio Setup

Add `accepted.fyi` as a clickable link in bio immediately — no follower requirement.

## TikTok Workarounds (Until 1K Followers)

| Method | How |
|--------|-----|
| **URL in bio text** | Write `accepted.fyi` in bio — not clickable but memorable |
| **Pin a comment** | Comment `accepted.fyi` on every video and pin it |
| **Say it in voiceover** | End every video with "accepted dot F-Y-I" |
| **Last slide** | Big text: `accepted.fyi` on final slide |
| **Instagram bridge** | Link Instagram in TikTok bio — put clickable link on Instagram |

## Influencer Outreach

### Finding Admissions Influencers

**On TikTok:**
- Search `#collegeadmissions`, `#collegeresults`, `#ivyday`
- Look for creators with 10K-100K followers (smaller creators are more likely to respond)
- Look for creators who react to admissions results, give advice, or share decisions

**On Instagram:**
- Same hashtag search — admissions coaches, student creators, "day in my life at [school]" accounts

**On YouTube:**
- Search "college decision reaction" or "how I got into Stanford"

### Outreach Message Template

```
Hey [name]! Love your admissions content.

I built accepted.fyi — it's basically Levels.fyi
for college admissions. 3,100+ real student outcomes
with GPA, SAT, extracurriculars, and decisions.

Thought your audience would find it useful —
especially with decision season coming up.

Happy to give you early access or do a collab
if you're interested!
```

### Outreach Tips

- Don't mass copy-paste — personalize the first line
- Offer something: "I can pull custom data for your audience"
- Start small — creators with 5K-20K followers are way more responsive
- Don't ask them to promote — ask if they find it useful
- Follow up once after 3-4 days, then move on

### For Students Who Just Posted Decisions

```
Congrats on [school]! You should add your result
to accepted.fyi — it helps other students see
what it takes to get in
```

## Platform Rules for DMs/Links

| Platform | Can You DM? | Can You Share Links? | Ban Risk |
|----------|------------|---------------------|----------|
| **Instagram** | Yes, DMs are open or requestable | Yes, links work in DMs | Low |
| **TikTok** | Limited — many creators restrict DMs | Links don't always work in DMs | Low |
| **Reddit** | Yes, but users hate unsolicited DMs | Links work but feel spammy | **High** |
| **Discord** | Yes, if you share a server | Links work | **Medium** |

### Best Approach Per Platform

- **Instagram** — best for influencer outreach. DMs are standard for business requests
- **TikTok** — comment on videos instead of DMing. Public comments are safer and other viewers see them
- **Reddit** — never DM unsolicited. Comment helpfully in threads and mention accepted.fyi naturally
- **Discord** — don't DM directly. Share in `#resources` channels after being active for a few days

## Discord Strategy

### Finding Admissions Discord Servers

1. Open Discord → Explore Public Servers (compass icon)
2. Search: `college admissions`, `applying to college`, `college prep`, `class of 2030`

### Useful Search Terms

| Search Term | What You'll Find |
|-------------|-----------------|
| `college admissions` | General admissions discussion servers |
| `class of 2030` | Current cycle students |
| `A2C` | r/ApplyingToCollege's Discord |
| `SAT` / `ACT` | Test prep servers |
| `ivy league` | School-specific servers |

### Sharing Without Getting Banned

- Don't spam links immediately after joining
- Be helpful for a few days first
- Share in `#resources` or `#self-promo` channels
- Ask a mod if unsure about self-promotion rules
- Frame it as a recommendation, not an ad:

> "hey I've been using this site accepted.fyi that has 3,100+ real admissions outcomes — you can filter by school, GPA, SAT. pretty useful for figuring out where you stand"

## Video Production Workflow

| Step | Tool | Cost |
|------|------|------|
| Design slides + transitions + timing | Canva (9:16 TikTok template) | Free |
| Combine slides + audio → export MP4 | Canva | Free |
| Add auto-captions | CapCut | Free |
| Add trending audio | TikTok (Add Sound on post screen) | Free |

### Design Guidelines

- Background: Solid dark (black or #0a0a0a)
- Font: Bold sans-serif (Inter, Montserrat)
- Text color: White, with red/green for emphasis on key numbers
- One message per slide — no paragraphs
- Duration: 2-3 seconds per slide
- Transitions: Dissolve, 0.3-0.5s
- Total video length: Under 30 seconds

### Caption Template

```
[Hook — shocking stat or question] [CTA] accepted.fyi

#collegeadmissions #classof2030 #decisionday #collegeresults #ivyleague #applyingtocollege #satscores
```

### Cover Image

Use the most shocking/scroll-stopping slide as the cover (e.g., "4.0 GPA. 1580 SAT. Rejected.")

### Posting Schedule

- Post between 6-9 PM EST (peak Gen Z hours)
- 1-2 videos per day during decision season
- Post same video on both TikTok and Instagram Reels
